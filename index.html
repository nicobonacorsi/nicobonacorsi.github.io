Central Limit Theorem ‚Äî intuition, hypotheses, and a quick proof sketch

Updated: Oct 30, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Tags: probability, convergence, CLT

If \(X_1, X_2, \ldots\) are i.i.d. with mean \(\mu\) and variance \(\sigma^2 \in (0,\infty)\), \[ \frac{1}{\sigma\sqrt{n}}\sum_{i=1}^n (X_i-\mu) \;\xRightarrow{d}\; \mathcal{N}(0,1). \] Intuitively, normalization balances the spread so that the cumulative effect of many small, weakly dependent ‚Äúkicks‚Äù approaches a Gaussian law.

Sketch: Expand the log-characteristic function via Taylor, control the remainder with Lyapunov or Lindeberg, and invoke L√©vy‚Äôs continuity theorem.

Takeaway: the CLT is a statement about the ‚Äúshape‚Äù of sums, not about convergence in probability of the sums themselves.

Modes of Convergence ‚Äî why they differ and how to navigate them

Updated: Oct 30, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Tags: convergence, measure-theory

We distinguish \(X_n \to X\) a.s., in probability, in \(L^p\), and in distribution: \[ X_n \xrightarrow{a.s.} X \;\Rightarrow\; X_n \xrightarrow{P} X \;\Rightarrow\; X_n \xRightarrow{d} X, \] while \(L^p\) sits orthogonally (dominated convergence bridges \(L^p\) to a.s.).

Useful trick: to show \(X_n \Rightarrow X\), it suffices to prove \(\mathbb{E}f(X_n)\to\mathbb{E}f(X)\) for all bounded continuous \(f\) (Portmanteau/DUC).

Beta‚ÄìGamma‚ÄìDirichlet ‚Äî the integral identities you‚Äôll use forever

Updated: Oct 30, 2025 ‚Ä¢ Tags: integrals, beta, gamma

The Beta integral \[ \mathrm{B}(\alpha,\beta)=\int_0^1 x^{\alpha-1}(1-x)^{\beta-1}\,dx = \frac{\Gamma(\alpha)\Gamma(\beta)}{\Gamma(\alpha+\beta)} \] extends to the Dirichlet integral over the simplex with the Jacobian trick and the Gamma change of variables. 

\[
\int_{\sum_{i=1}^k x_i=1,\, x_i\ge0}\prod_{i=1}^k x_i^{\alpha_i-1}\,dx
= \frac{\prod_{i=1}^k \Gamma(\alpha_i)}{\Gamma\!\left(\sum_{i=1}^k \alpha_i\right)}.
\]

Proof by induction on \(k\) or via stick-breaking; both reveal the conjugacy structure ubiquitous in Bayesian models.
