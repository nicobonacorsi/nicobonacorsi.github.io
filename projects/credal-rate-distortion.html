Abstract

Classical rate–distortion theory starts from a single probability law. This project asks what changes when the source is described by a credal set: a family of plausible probability distributions representing model ambiguity, epistemic uncertainty, or adversarial misspecification.

Research question

How should lossy compression be defined when the encoder and decoder do not trust a unique source distribution? The guiding question is whether there exists a useful rate–distortion theory whose feasibility, distortion, and coding structure are robust over an entire family of distributions.

Key idea

The key idea is to replace a single expected distortion constraint with credal admissibility: a code is acceptable only if it respects the relevant distortion requirement throughout the uncertainty set.

Mathematical formulation

A minimal prototype is the following. Let $\mathcal P$ be a credal set over a source alphabet $\mathcal X$, and let $d : \mathcal X \times \widehat{\mathcal X} \to \mathbb R_+$ be a distortion function.

$$ R_{\mathcal P}(D) = \inf_{P_{\widehat X|X}} I_{\mathcal P}(X;\widehat X) \quad \text{subject to} \quad \sup_{P \in \mathcal P} \mathbb E_{P P_{\widehat X|X}} \big[d(X,\widehat X)\big] \le D. $$
