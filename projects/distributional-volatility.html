Abstract

This study forecasts annualized variance over the next 22 trading days. Labels enter estimation only after their complete target windows have matured. Six experts are combined quarterly through bounded-loss Hedge aggregation, and a 90% upper forecast is calibrated across 22 interlaced residual streams with non-overlapping target windows within each stream.

Over 2013–2026, the ensemble reduces QLIKE by 3.34% and log-RMSE by 14.94% relative to raw VIX variance. The corresponding HAC p-values are 0.608 for QLIKE loss differences and \(3.20\times10^{-15}\) for squared log-error differences. Empirical upper-bound coverage is 89.03%. The upper-forecast controller reaches 8.30% annualized volatility and an 11.56% maximum drawdown, compared with 16.81% and 33.72% for SPY buy-and-hold; standard VIX targeting retains the stronger risk-adjusted return.

Research design

The two studies in the full report follow the same quantitative architecture: estimate uncertainty, preserve temporal causality, and embed the estimate directly in the decision rule. In the volatility model, matured forecast errors determine both expert weights and the upper variance forecast used for exposure control.

The empirical design combines fixed development, validation, and evaluation periods, explicit transaction-cost accounting, dependence-aware inference, benchmark strategies, and machine-checkable temporal audits.

Data and accounting conventions

The volatility study uses SPX closes, Cboe VIX history, SPY adjusted-close total returns, and the FRED three-month Treasury constant-maturity yield. Raw snapshots and SHA-256 hashes are included in the accompanying repository.

Uninvested capital earns the Treasury proxy and leverage above one is financed at the same rate. This common-rate financing convention isolates the forecasting and exposure mechanisms from broker-specific funding schedules.

Target and information set

At forecast origin \(t\), the target is annualized variance over the next 22 trading days:

\[ RV^{(22)}_{t,+} = \frac{252}{22} \sum_{j=1}^{22}r_{t+j}^2. \tag{9} \]

Every origin stores the final date of its target window. A historical label from origin \(s\) may enter a model refit or residual calibration at date \(t\) only when

\[ \operatorname{target\_end}(s)

The strict inequality prevents the current close from entering a target used at that same close. This restriction is enforced in emitted audit files and in unit tests.

Expert forecasts and bounded-loss Hedge

The forecast panel contains six experts:

  1. ridge regression using log VIX variance;
  2. ridge regression using VIX and HAR-style 5-, 22-, and 66-day variance features;
  3. ridge regression adding downside-return and VIX-change features;
  4. raw VIX variance;
  5. trailing 22-day variance;
  6. EWMA variance with decay 0.94.

Ridge penalties and Duan smearing factors are selected for each regression expert on 2005–2008. Models are then refit quarterly on expanding samples of fully matured labels.

For realized variance \(y\) and forecast \(\widehat y\), the QLIKE loss is

\[ \ell(y,\widehat y) = \frac{y}{\widehat y} - \log\left( \frac{y}{\widehat y} \right) - 1. \tag{11} \]

The loss cap \(L=4.738868\) is fixed as the 99th percentile of pooled expert losses on the development period. At each quarterly update, the latest \(n\le504\) matured observations are used. With \(K=6\),

\[ \widetilde\ell_{k,s} = \min\{\ell_{k,s},L\}, \qquad \eta_t = \sqrt{ \frac{8\log K}{nL^2} }, \tag{12} \] \[ \pi_{k,t} = \frac{ \exp\left( -\eta_t\sum_s\widetilde\ell_{k,s} \right) }{ \sum_{j=1}^K \exp\left( -\eta_t\sum_s\widetilde\ell_{j,s} \right) }, \qquad \widehat v_t = \sum_{k=1}^K \pi_{k,t}\widehat v_{k,t}. \tag{13} \]

The two-year memory allows the ensemble to adapt across volatility regimes. The learning rate is determined analytically by the bounded-loss Hedge expression.

Quarterly Hedge weights. The aggregation rule reallocates weight gradually as relative expert performance changes.

Interlaced upper-forecast calibration

Define the log residual

\[ e_s = \log RV^{(22)}_{s,+} - \log\widehat v_s. \tag{14} \]

Forecast origins are divided into 22 streams according to their index modulo 22. Consecutive origins in a stream are 22 trading days apart, so their forward target windows do not overlap. At origin \(t\), calibration uses at most the latest 63 matured residuals in the same stream:

\[ h(s)=h(t), \qquad \operatorname{target\_end}(s)

The 90% upper forecast is

\[ U_t = \widehat v_t \exp\left\{ Q_{0.90}(e_s) \right\}. \tag{16} \]

The interlaced design is selected as the primary calibration method because every within-stream residual sample is built from disjoint forward windows. A pooled residual estimator achieves lower validation pinball loss, while the interlaced estimator offers cleaner dependence control for horizon-overlapping targets.

The interlaced construction addresses target overlap; it is not presented as a finite-sample conformal guarantee under market nonstationarity.

Point forecasts, realized forward variance, and the calibrated 90% upper forecast. The upper series widens sharply in stressed periods.

Economic decision rule

At the close of day \(t\), the next-day SPY exposure is

\[ \ell_t = \operatorname{clip} \left( \frac{0.12}{\sqrt{U_t}}, 0.2, 1.5 \right). \tag{17} \]

Let \(R^{SPY}_{t+1}\) be the next adjusted-close total return and \(R_t^f\) the daily Treasury proxy. With one basis point per unit change in exposure,

\[ R^p_{t+1} = \ell_tR^{SPY}_{t+1} + (1-\ell_t)R_t^f - 10^{-4}|\ell_t-\ell_{t-1}|. \tag{18} \]

Benchmarks include buy-and-hold, standard VIX targeting, point-forecast targeting, constant exposure fixed from validation, and a VIX rule scaled using validation-period mean exposure. An equal-exposure VIX series isolates timing effects from average market exposure.

Reproducibility and audit controls

The repository regenerates every reported table and figure from immutable public-data snapshots in a Python 3.12 environment through a single execution script. The audit suite verifies:

  • SHA-256 verification of all five raw data snapshots;
  • feasibility tests for capped-simplex projections and selected portfolio weights;
  • an active-set QP objective comparison against SLSQP at \(10^{-9}\) tolerance;
  • positive-semidefinite and trace-inflation checks for bootstrap covariance matrices;
  • continuity and completeness checks for the 25-portfolio panel;
  • strict future-target and label-maturity checks;
  • overlap checks requiring calibration observations to come from the same interlaced stream with disjoint target windows;
  • accounting identities for all-cash and all-SPY strategies;
  • non-empty artifact checks for every generated output.

All ten integrity tests pass, and the raw-data manifest verifies every snapshot without a hash mismatch. The tests cover data continuity, optimization feasibility, temporal causality, calibration overlap, accounting identities, and artifact generation.

Scope and research extensions

The volatility experiment uses daily squared returns as the forward-variance proxy. Intraday realized kernels, HARQ measurement-error corrections, option-surface features, and point-in-time variance-risk-premium measures would sharpen both the target and the feature set.

The interlaced construction controls overlap within calibration streams; regime-conditioned residual models offer the next step toward conditional coverage. An execution-oriented extension would implement exposure through liquid index futures with observed funding, bid–ask spreads, and roll costs. A pre-specified evaluation on an additional market or forward period would then measure portability of expert weights, calibration quantiles, and exposure mapping.

Conclusion

The volatility study combines maturity-safe expert aggregation with non-overlapping residual calibration. The ensemble materially improves log-scale forecast accuracy relative to raw VIX variance, and the calibrated upper forecast supports an exposure rule with substantially lower volatility and drawdown than buy-and-hold.

VIX targeting remains the stronger benchmark on risk-adjusted return. This locates the contribution in distributional forecasting, temporal integrity, and downside-sensitive control rather than universal economic dominance.
