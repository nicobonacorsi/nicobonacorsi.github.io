Spectral Methods for High-Dimensional Prediction and Face Recognition

Nicolò Bonacorsi

Question. How can PCA and SVD reduce high-dimensional crime-rate and face-image data while preserving predictive and recognition structure?

Key idea. Replace raw high-dimensional observations by coordinates in low-rank spectral subspaces: principal components for crime data and eigenfaces for image data.

Results. Principal-component regression improves adjusted $R^2$ from 0.5544 to 0.8555, while the eigenfaces classifier reaches 98.3333% accuracy on the Olivetti dataset.

Credal Rate–Distortion Theory

Nicolò Bonacorsi

Question. How should rate–distortion theory change when the source is not described by one probability law, but by a credal set of plausible distributions?

Key idea. Replace a single probabilistic distortion constraint with uncertainty-aware admissibility over families of distributions, revealing a geometry of robust compression.

Results. The project studies credal distortion, admissible codebooks, graph-coloring compression regimes, and finite-window phenomena between local and global uncertainty.

Bayesian Modeling of Collatz Stopping Times

Nicolò Bonacorsi

Question. Can Collatz stopping times be modeled as structured random variables with arithmetic regimes, heavy tails, and calibrated predictive uncertainty?

Key idea. Treat stopping times as overdispersed count data and use Bayesian hierarchical modeling to capture residue-class effects and tail behavior.

Results. The project builds a Bayesian pipeline for stopping-time data, including regime-conditioned models, Monte Carlo calibration, posterior checks, and distributional diagnostics.
